The sunflower seed was introduced in Spain by the colonizers and later spread to the rest of Europe, it was cultivated for more than two centuries in Spain and in the rest of Europe for its ornamental value, due to the size and especially the beauty of its inflorescences and it was during the 19th century when the industrial exploitation of its oil for food began.

Sunflower cultivation is carried out under both direct sowing and tillage practices. When choosing a plot it is necessary to identify the presence of hardened horizons (plow floors, tosca, etc.) that can affect the normal exploration of the roots. The deeper the soil, the greater the chances of reaching high yields. Its cultivation in soils with thicknesses less than 40 cm is not recommended. Maximum yields are achieved in deep soils. The fallow period should be extended for at least 45 days before sowing to allow adequate accumulation of water and supply of nutrients.

The optimal time for sowing sunflower varies according to the production regions. Sunflowers

Sunflowers need a lot of sunlight to grow and should be planted in spacious areas in order to grow as much as possible.

The most important characteristic, and for which these flowers are named, is their ability to follow the path of the sun, as they rotate in search of sunlight.
